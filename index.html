What is good design?

There are two different kinds of good design that I can think of, but there is definitely more than that. The first one is design that is accessible to its users/viewers. The design offers itself to be used/viewed efficiently by all different kinds of people, such as those who may or may not suffer from a disability. Can those who are colorblind access it with the same efficiency as those who aren't? Question like those should be answered with a yes if it is good design. Another good design relies on the aesthetics that please the viewer. Everyone has a different interpretation of what good design is. For example, I hate the UI of Photoshop, I don't find it to be user-friendly, yet it is the industry standard so many others probably find it to be good design despite me thinking otherwise. It differs from person to person, but keep in mind that this version of "good design" is just based on aesthetics. There are different categories of good design. Good design can range from accessibility to aesthetics to efficiency to usability and so on.
